Chicago Family Housing Community

We are creating a community that is:

  • for families with children and people without children who want to live in a community with children
  • intentionally inclusive of people of different races, classes, genders, sexual orientations, and other social identities
  • based in a building with four or more units and a backyard
  • affordable for people who can pay around $1,500 in monthly housing costs (specific units may be more or less depending on size)

Like a typical apartment or condo, our building will have individual units with their own kitchens, bathrooms, and so on.

Unlike most housing, we are creating a community, by:

  • welcoming children into each others' units to play with each other
  • sharing childcare with each other, both informally (e.g. your child wanders over to my unit to play with my child) and formally (e.g. I babysit for you when you have class Monday nights, and you babysit for me when I have band practice Thursdays)
  • making community decisions collaboratively, such as how to use the yard and other common spaces
  • working together to maintain common spaces and the building
  • planning community events, like building-wide dinners and block parties
  • exploring other ways to support each other and enjoy living together

Ownership of the building will be structured as a limited equity co-op. This means that:

  • all of the residents of the co-op will be its legal owners
  • monthly payments will go towards the building's mortgage and upkeep
  • unlike a rental, if at some point you wish to leave the co-op, you can receive back at least some of what you've paid
  • unlike a condo, you do not need to make a large down payment to move in
  • unlike a rental or a condo, if somebody moves out, the other residents will decide who moves in
  • unlike a condo, the value, or "equity," of your unit is limited to its value when you move in (plus inflation and any substantial improvements you make)

This project is being started by Ali and Michael, who are both 35, and Fern, who is 6 months.
